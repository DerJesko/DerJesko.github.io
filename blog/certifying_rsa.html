Certified RSA Permutations

The amazing work of [FLS] showed how to build non-interactive zero-knowledge arguments for all of NP from trapdoor permutations. Later, it was noticed [BY,G11,GR,CL] that FLS implicitly assumed a few properties about trapdoor permutations that the canidates for trapdoor permutations did not have. These problems have been rectified in the papers that notice these issues.

For anyone reading this: In the middle of writing this article I discovered that my observation that RSA with an encryption exponent e that is prime and bigger than N is a certified trapdoor permutation is already in a footnote of [GR], which is I never finished this article.
